The Beginning of Haiti

Haiti was “found” by Christopher Columbus while he was on his voyage on December 4, 1482 (the date is a bit unclear since there are different dates reported). He would name it the “Hispaniola” and soon begin the separation and mistreatment of the country.

“During the early colonial period, Hispaniola’s key location on the northern edge of the Caribbean Sea made the colony the logistical base for Spain’s conquest of most of the Western Hemisphere. Columbus thereby initiated an era of exploration, subjugation, and colonization that lasted for centuries. The explorer has been accused by many historians of initiating the genocide of Hispaniola’s indigenous Arawak population, and he is also considered by some to be the originator of the transatlantic slave trade.”

Around the year 1697, the French would take over Haiti as the Spaniards kept the Dominican Republic. The French would then bring in Slaves from Africa to Haiti causing a mixture of people with different backgrounds. Since then Haiti has continue to grow, although the Dominican Republic and Haiti remains separte eventhough they are on the same island.
