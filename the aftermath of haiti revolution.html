The Aftermath of Haiti Revolution

Much more went on during the revolution and after as the French broke the armistice in May 1802. Other leaders stepped up in the place of l’Overture and continued the war until they won. Haitian independence day was declared on January 1st, 1804 and to celebrate this, Haitians will eat soup joumou keeping the tradition and appreciation alive. The revolution was a success as Haiti won against the French and was essentially freed and independent. When news hit that Haiti won and became the first Black freed country, it showed other countries mainly in the Caribbean that they too can do the same and Haiti extended their hand to help. Haiti helped free Venezuela, Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Panama from the Spaniard's control.
