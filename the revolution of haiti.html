The Revolution of Haiti

After years of enslavement, Haitians had enough of it. They wanted to become independent and the only way to achieve this is by defeating its master, the French. In 1791 the rebellion was initiated. Toussaint l’Overture, was the first slave in Haiti to act on the rebellion against planters on August 21, 1791. Within a year, they controlled a third of the island and were growing strong and fast and couldn’t be stopped at this point. By 1801 l’Overture revolution expanded beyond Haiti conquering the Dominican Republic as well. He abolished slavery and “declared himself Governor-General for life over the entire island of Hispaniola.”
